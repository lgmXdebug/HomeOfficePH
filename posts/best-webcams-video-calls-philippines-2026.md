## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### 1. Resolution
**720p** is the minimum for video calls. **1080p** is the sweet spot — looks sharp on any screen without massive file sizes. 4K is overkill for most video calls.

### 2. Frame Rate
**30fps** is standard for video calls. **60fps** makes motion smoother — better if you move around a lot during calls or do any screen recording.

### 3. Autofocus
Fixed-focus webcams (like the C270) are fine if you sit in the same spot every call. If you move around, get one with autofocus.

### 4. Low Light Performance
This is critical for Philippine homes. Many condos and apartments have poor lighting. Look for webcams with **auto light correction** or **low light optimization**.

### 5. Built-in Microphone
Most webcams have built-in mics — good enough for casual calls. For professional calls or recordings, pair your webcam with a dedicated USB microphone.

## FAQs

**Q: Do I really need a webcam if my laptop has one?**
Yes. Built-in laptop cameras are typically 720p with poor low-light performance, fixed focus, and bad angles (usually looking up at you). An external webcam sits at eye level and looks significantly better.

**Q: Where can I buy webcams in the Philippines?**
Lazada and Shopee are your best options. Both have official Logitech stores. Also check Villman, PC Express, and Gilmore IT Center for in-store options.

**Q: Is the Logitech C920 worth it over the C270?**
If you're on frequent video calls or want to look professional, yes. The difference in image quality is noticeable, especially on larger screens.

**Q: Do I need a webcam if I have a good phone?**
You can use your phone as a webcam (via DroidCam or Camo), but it's inconvenient and drains your battery. A dedicated webcam is much simpler.
