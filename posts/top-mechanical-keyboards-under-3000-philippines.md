## Switch Guide: Which Switch Should You Choose?

This is the most confusing part for first-time mechanical keyboard buyers. Here's a simple breakdown:

### Red Switches — Silent & Fast
- Light actuation, no tactile bump, quiet
- Best for: gaming, office/condo use, light typists
- Sound: soft thud

### Brown Switches — The Middle Ground
- Light tactile bump, quiet-ish
- Best for: typing and gaming mix, shared spaces
- Sound: soft click with slight bump

### Blue Switches — Clicky & Loud
- Strong tactile bump, audible click
- Best for: typists who love feedback, private rooms
- Sound: loud click — NOT condo/office friendly
- ⚠️ Do NOT use blue switches in a shared space

## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### 1. Layout
- **60%** — most compact, no arrow keys, takes adjustment
- **75%** — compact but keeps arrow keys and F-row (best for most people)
- **TKL** — full keyboard minus numpad, most familiar layout
- **Full size** — all keys including numpad, takes the most desk space

### 2. Hot-Swap vs Soldered
Hot-swap means you can pull out and replace switches without soldering. Great for beginners who want to experiment. The RK61 and Keychron K2 both offer hot-swap versions.

### 3. Wired vs Wireless
Wireless is more convenient but costs more. For a fixed desk setup, wired is totally fine.

### 4. Switch Brand
- **Gateron** — smooth, affordable, widely available
- **Cherry MX** — the original, reliable, slightly stiffer
- **Outemu** — budget option, less smooth than Gateron

## FAQs

**Q: Are mechanical keyboards worth it for office work?**
Yes — especially if you type for hours daily. The tactile feedback reduces typing errors and the durability means your keyboard will last 10+ years vs 2–3 years for membrane keyboards.

**Q: Are mechanical keyboards too loud for condo living?**
Depends on the switch. Red and Brown switches are quiet enough for most condos. Avoid Blue switches if you have thin walls or roommates.

**Q: Where can I buy mechanical keyboards in the Philippines?**
Shopee and Lazada have the best selection. For in-store, try Gilmore IT Center in Cubao, Villman, or PC Express.

**Q: Can I use a mechanical keyboard for both work and gaming?**
Yes — especially the RK61 and Keychron K2. Both work great for typing and gaming.
