## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### 1. Desktop Size
For a full work setup (monitor, laptop, keyboard, mouse), you need at least **100x50cm**. The sweet spot is **120x60cm** — fits everything without taking over the room.

### 2. Electric vs Manual
Electric desks are worth the extra cost. Manual cranks are inconvenient and most people stop using the height adjustment after the first week — defeating the whole purpose of a standing desk.

### 3. Weight Capacity
Make sure your desk can handle your setup. A monitor, laptop, and accessories can weigh 20–30kg. Look for at least **50kg capacity**.

### 4. Height Range
For Filipino heights (average 5'2"–5'8"), look for a height range of **58–120cm minimum**. This covers sitting and standing positions for most people.

### 5. Stability
Wobble is the enemy of productivity. Dual-motor desks like the FlexiSpot are significantly more stable than single-motor options. Test it if you can — push the desk slightly at standing height and see if it shakes.

## FAQs

**Q: Are standing desks worth it in the Philippines?**
Yes — especially with our sedentary WFH culture. Alternating between sitting and standing reduces back pain, improves posture, and boosts energy levels during long work days.

**Q: How long should I stand per day?**
Start with 30 minutes of standing per 2 hours of sitting. Build up gradually. Don't stand all day — that's just as bad as sitting all day.

**Q: Where can I buy standing desks in the Philippines?**
Lazada, Shopee, and IKEA Philippines are your best options. Some brands like FlexiSpot also have official Shopee stores with local warranty support.

**Q: Is ₱18,000 too much for a standing desk?**
Consider it a long-term health investment. A good standing desk lasts 8–10 years. That's ₱1,800/year — cheaper than most monthly gym memberships.
