## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### 1. Size
Bigger = more light. For dark Philippine rooms, go with at least a **12" ring light**. 18" is ideal if you have the space.

### 2. Color Temperature
Look for adjustable color temperature between **3200K (warm)** and **5500K (cool daylight)**. This lets you match your room's existing lighting for the most natural look.

### 3. Brightness Levels
More levels = more control. Look for at least 10 brightness levels.

### 4. Mount Type
- **Tripod stand** — most common, takes floor space, very adjustable
- **Desk clamp** — saves floor space, limited height adjustment
- **Clip-on** — most compact, least light output

### 5. Power Source
Most ring lights use AC adapters — fine for a fixed desk setup. Battery-powered options exist but are more expensive and less bright.

## Placement Tips for Best Results

Getting the right placement is just as important as buying the right ring light.

**Position:** Place the ring light directly in front of you, at eye level or slightly above.

**Distance:** About 60–90cm from your face. Too close = harsh shadows. Too far = not enough light.

**Angle:** Tilt slightly downward (15 degrees) for the most flattering angle.

**Background:** Turn off harsh ceiling lights behind you. Let the ring light be your primary light source.

## FAQs

**Q: Do I need a ring light if I have a window?**
If you can position your desk facing the window, natural light is actually better than a ring light. But natural light is inconsistent — it changes throughout the day and disappears at night. A ring light gives you consistent light anytime.

**Q: What size ring light is best for video calls?**
10"–18" is the sweet spot. Under 10" doesn't produce enough light for dark rooms. Over 18" is overkill for video calls.

**Q: Can I use a ring light for YouTube or content creation?**
Yes — especially the Neewer 18" and Godox LR120. Both produce enough light quality for video content.
