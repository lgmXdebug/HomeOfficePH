## ANC vs Passive Noise Isolation: What's the Difference?

**Active Noise Cancellation (ANC):**
Uses microphones to detect ambient sound and generates an opposite sound wave to cancel it out. Best for steady, low-frequency sounds like aircon, traffic, and engines.

**Passive Noise Isolation:**
Physical blocking of sound through ear cup padding. Better for sudden, high-frequency sounds like voices and knocks.

The best headphones for WFH use **both** — Sony's XM5 combines excellent ANC with plush ear cups for maximum isolation.

## Buying Guide: What to Look For

### 1. ANC Quality
Not all ANC is created equal. Sony and Bose lead the industry. Anker offers the best budget ANC. Avoid cheap "ANC" headphones under ₱1,000 — the ANC is usually cosmetic.

### 2. Microphone Quality
Critical for WFH. Look for headphones with dedicated boom microphones or multiple built-in mics with noise filtering. The Jabra Evolve2 55 and Sony XM5 lead here.

### 3. Battery Life
For WFH use, 20+ hours is the minimum. The Anker Q45's 50-hour battery means charging once a week.

### 4. Comfort for Long Wear
You'll wear these 8+ hours a day. Look for memory foam ear cups, adjustable headband, and lightweight design. Sony's XM5 at 250g is remarkably light for an over-ear headphone.

### 5. Wired Option
Make sure your headphones can be used wired (via 3.5mm cable) as a backup when battery dies during an important call.

**Q: Are noise-cancelling headphones worth it for WFH in the Philippines?**
Absolutely. Philippine home environments are notoriously noisy — street noise, family members, neighbors. A good ANC headphone can add 2–3 hours of focused work to your day.

**Q: Sony XM5 vs XM4 — which should I buy?**
XM4 if you want to save ₱5,000 and portability (folds flat). XM5 if you want the best call quality and don't mind the price.

**Q: Where can I buy noise-cancelling headphones in the Philippines?**

**Q: Can I use ANC headphones without music?**
Yes — you can use ANC in "transparency" or "ambient" mode to reduce noise without music playing. Great for focus work without audio.
